Welcome to Sustainable Brands, and thanks for your efforts to tackle the big issues! Protecting the Canadian boreal forest is critical to fight climate change and protect biodiversity. Unfortunately, the forest is being flushed down the toilet by Procter & Gamble for its flagship TP brand Charmin.

While Charmin’s website is full of claims about protecting forests, and its cute Charmin bears espouse the virtues of soft toilet paper and “enjoying the go,” Charmin is being called out for greenwashing and P&G needs to step up to make Charmin a brand for good.

C’mon Procter and Gamble, it’s not rocket science:

  1. Stop using pulp from primary and endangered forests and reduce fiber from natural forests
  2. Only use FSC chain-of-custody certified fiber
  3. Add recycled and alternative fiber to Charmin toilet paper.
